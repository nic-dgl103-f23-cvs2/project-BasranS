"A Legacy of Lifeline in Comox Valley"

The Comox Valley Lifeline Society is a non-profit, charitable organization that provides Lifeline services to a variety of individuals who may feel reassured that help is available to them twenty four hours a day, seven days a week. The Society was established in 1988 by the 'Lions of the North Island' because the membership believed that people deserved the dignity to live on their own and have access to immediate help in an emergency situation Since its inception, the Society has evolved alongside advancements in medical alert systems. Initially starting with basic emergency response services,the Lifeline system has continuously adapted and integrated cutting-edge technology to ensure swift and reliable assistance for its members. Over the years, the Comox Valley Lifeline Society has forged partnerships with local healthcare providers, emergency services, and caregivers, fostering a network dedicated to enhancing the safety and well-being of the community's residents. Through unwavering commitment and dedication, the Society has expanded its services to meet the evolving needs of individuals seeking independence and security while residing in the Comox Valley.
